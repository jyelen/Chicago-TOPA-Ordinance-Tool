A practical guide to Chicago's TOPA pilot programs, cooperative conversion, and financial tools for tenant groupsGuía práctica sobre los programas piloto TOPA de Chicago y la conversión cooperativa

Chicago faces a widening affordable housing gap. Decades of disinvestment followed by rapid reinvestment have displaced tens of thousands of long-term residents — with the heaviest losses in historically Black and Latine neighborhoods on the northwest and south sides. Between 2012 and 2021, the Chicago metro lost hundreds of thousands of units affordable to low-income renters.

Preservation is among the most cost-effective anti-displacement strategies. Keeping existing affordable rental housing in community hands — through tenant purchase, cooperative ownership, community land trusts, or nonprofit stewardship — avoids the higher costs of new construction while protecting residents already in place.

Tenant Opportunity to Purchase (TOPA) gives renters a structured right to purchase their building before it can be sold to an outside investor. When paired with the right resources — capital, public subsidies, legal assistance, and technical expertise — TOPA can be a powerful tool for resident-led preservation. Washington D.C.'s TOPA program, active since 1980, has helped preserve tens of thousands of affordable units and supported dozens of resident-owned cooperatives. Chicago's pilots are a meaningful start — but making them work requires sustained investment in tenant education, acquisition financing, and organizational capacity.

Chicago enfrenta una brecha creciente de vivienda asequible. Entre 2012 y 2021, el área metropolitana perdió cientos de miles de unidades asequibles para inquilinos de bajos ingresos, con las mayores pérdidas en vecindarios históricamente negros y latinos.

La preservación es una de las estrategias anti-desplazamiento más rentables. Mantener la vivienda asequible existente en manos comunitarias evita los costos más altos de la nueva construcción mientras protege a los residentes.

El TOPA otorga a los inquilinos el derecho estructurado de comprar su edificio antes de que se venda a un inversionista externo. El programa de Washington D.C., vigente desde 1980, ha preservado decenas de miles de unidades asequibles.

What is a limited-equity housing cooperative (LEHC)? A limited-equity co-op is a resident-owned building where members collectively own the corporation that holds the property. Each member purchases a share giving them the right to occupy their unit and participate in building governance. Unlike market-rate condos, LEHCs place restrictions on how much shares can appreciate over time — keeping the housing permanently affordable for future residents while still allowing members to build modest equity. Members pay a monthly carrying charge instead of rent; this charge covers the building's mortgage, operating costs, and reserves. Because members collectively control the building, they are protected from displacement by outside investors and have a direct say in how their building is managed. ¿Qué es una cooperativa de vivienda de capital limitado? Una cooperativa de capital limitado es un edificio de propiedad de los residentes donde los miembros poseen colectivamente la corporación que mantiene la propiedad. Cada miembro compra una acción que le da derecho a ocupar su unidad y participar en la gobernanza del edificio. A diferencia de los condominios de mercado, las cooperativas de capital limitado restringen cuánto pueden apreciarse las acciones con el tiempo, manteniendo la vivienda permanentemente asequible para futuros residentes. Los miembros pagan una cuota mensual en lugar de renta que cubre la hipoteca del edificio, costos operativos y reservas.

Co-op Mortgage Sizing — Conceptual NoteDimensionamiento Hipotecario Cooperativo — Nota Conceptual

In a rental property, the mortgage is sized based on net operating income (NOI = rents − expenses), with DSCR measuring how well that income covers debt service. In a housing co-op, there is no independent rent income — the carrying charge covers mortgage payments, operating costs, and reserves in one. This tool therefore works backwards from affordability:En una propiedad de alquiler, la hipoteca se dimensiona basándose en el NOI. En una cooperativa, no hay ingresos de renta independientes — la cuota cubre todo. Esta herramienta trabaja hacia atrás desde la asequibilidad:

  1. Set target carrying charge: What can members realistically afford? (Compared to AMI benchmarks.)Establecer cuota objetivo: ¿Qué pueden pagar los miembros de manera realista?
  2. Subtract operating costs: Property taxes + insurance + maintenance + reserves + utilities + training = total annual operating costs.Restar costos operativos: Impuestos + seguros + mantenimiento + reservas + servicios + capacitación.
  3. Available for debt service: Total annual carrying charges − operating costs = max annual debt service.Disponible para deuda: Cuotas anuales totales − costos operativos = servicio de deuda máximo anual.
  4. DSCR constraint: Divide available debt service by minimum DSCR to get max allowable debt service, then size the loan accordingly.Restricción de DSCR: Dividir el servicio de deuda disponible por el DSCR mínimo para obtener el servicio de deuda máximo permitido, luego dimensionar el préstamo.
  5. LTV constraint: Also check that the loan doesn't exceed the LTV limit against the purchase price. Take the lower of the two.Restricción de LTV: Verificar que el préstamo no exceda el límite de LTV. Tomar el menor de los dos.

The remaining gap (total project cost − mortgage − share equity) represents the subsidy, grants, or soft equity that must be raised from public or philanthropic sources.La brecha restante (costo total − hipoteca − capital de acciones) representa el subsidio o capital blando que debe obtenerse de fuentes públicas o filantrópicas.
